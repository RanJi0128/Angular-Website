Major League Lacrosse

The Major League Lacrosse is a semi-professional field lacrosse league, not to be confused with the National Lacrosse League which is a semi-professional box lacrosse league. Field lacrosse is an outdoor contact sport with ten players on each team. Each team has three attackmen, three midfielders, three defensemen, and one goalie. There are nine teams in the MLL, who play 14 regular season games starting in the spring and continuing into the summer. After the regular season ends there is a four-team playoff for the Steinfeld Cup. Lacrosse is a relatively new sport in America but the institution of the MLL has helped grow its popularity across the country making lacrosse tickets a hot commodity. Make sure to get your 2018 Major League Lacrosse tickets today to see the action live!

PGA Tour Golf

The game of golf first began in Scotland in the 15th century. It has withstood over time and remains to be one of the most popular sports in the world. Whether it be for leisure or watching the pros play, golf is a sport that everyone can participate in. And watching the pros play surely is a spectacle so make sure to get your tickets to a professional golf match to see the action live.

There are four major championships in men’s professional golf, otherwise known as the majors. Of these four championships are the Masters Tournament, U.S. Open, The Open Championship, and the PGA Championship. The Masters Tournament is played at the beautiful Augusta National Golf Club in Georgia every year. The U.S. Open and PGA Championship are played at various course in the United Sates and the Open Championship is played at various course in the United Kingdom.

Any golf course hosting the event proves to be just as picturesque and difficult as the next.

NASCAR

The National Association for Stock Car Auto Racing (NASCAR) is a privately owned American auto racing company. It is best known for stock car racing which is a term used for automobiles that are designed specifically for racing. NASCAR was founded in 1948 by William France Sr. and the current CEO is his grandson, Brian France. NASCAR’s three largest series are the Monster Energy Cup, the Xfinity Series, and the Camping World Truck Series. The Cup Series is the highest level of competition within NASCAR. It has had numerous title sponsors over the years beginning with R.J. Reynolds Tobacco Company in 1972. Monster Energy became the title sponsor beginning in 2017.

NASCAR events are extremely thrilling to watch so make sure to get your NASCAR tickets to catch all the racing action live!
